Vulture

Free Food. Simplified.

⬇

By MIT. For MIT.
Vulture is a free food web app to replace the
current free food mailing lists. Currently, MIT students
subscribe to mailing lists where anyone can email
out information about free food. However, this system
has its flaws. Since so many people subscribe to the
mailing lists, they are a target for spam. Additionally,
there is no way to know when the food is gone;
thus, students may go to the location of the free
food and discover that there is nothing left. Our
website solves these issues and more!

How it works
The site displays real-time information about free food on campus.
A map shows the locations of various free foods. The sortable table
allows you to easily find what you are looking for. On the
top left, you can view details about the food posting based on
the selected map marker or the listing in the table, and report
if the food is gone. To post food, users can login with
Google authentication and post food type, location, quantity, etc.
The login deters individuals from posting spam.
